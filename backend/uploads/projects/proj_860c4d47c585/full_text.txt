sovs.xyz
Enable individuals to declare personal
sovereignty on-chain.
Sovs.xyz is a project launched by Consensys CEO and Ethereum co-founder Joe Lubin at Devcon 2024 in Bangkok. The platform invites people to make a
permanent, on-chain declaration of their personal sovereignty on the Linea blockchain, a zero-knowledge rollup compatible with Ethereum. Users visit the site,
select the forms of sovereignty that matter most to them, such as financial sovereignty, data ownership, freedom from centralized tech platforms, or ecological
stewardship, and then sign an attestation that gets recorded permanently on-chain. The project is rooted in the idea that living life on-chain is itself an act of
declaring personal independence from centralized systems. When enough sovereign individuals come together voluntarily around shared values, they form
communities that are more resilient and more fair than what traditional institutions tend to produce. Lubin has framed sovs.xyz as a small but meaningful step
toward a decentralized future, where network states and digitally organized communities replace or complement legacy governance structures. The concept of
network states was popularized by tech investor Balaji Srinivasan, who described them as tightly connected online communities that can pool resources, acquire
territory, and eventually seek recognition from existing nations. Sovs.xyz does not itself govern anything or manage territory. It is a symbolic and practical starting
point, a public record of individuals who have chosen to state, in a way that cannot be erased or altered, that they believe in self-determination. The attestations
cover areas including control over personal assets without reliance on banks, control over how personal data is collected and used, access to open-source and
permissionless networks, and a recognition that personal sovereignty depends on the health of natural ecosystems. The platform is free to use and the attestation
process is quick, requiring only a connection to the Linea network.
MoonDAO
To fund, collaborate, and compete on
challenges that get us closer to a lunar
settlement.
MoonDAO is a decentralized organization that funds space research, missions, and technology development with the goal of establishing a permanent settlement
on the Moon. Founded in 2021, the organization raised over 2,600 ETH through its community and operates entirely through on-chain governance. Members hold
a token called $MOONEY, which grants voting rights on proposals, budgets, and project selection. There is no central leadership making unilateral decisions.
Every allocation of funds is visible on the blockchain. The organization has already produced notable results. In 2022, MoonDAO became the first decentralized
organization to send a person to space when its members voted to purchase a seat on a Blue Origin flight for Coby Cotton of the YouTube channel Dude Perfect.
Since then, the community has allocated over $600,000 to more than 75 space-related projects, ranging from zero-gravity astronaut training programs to
early-stage research on lunar infrastructure components. MoonDAO's roadmap targets an operational lunar settlement by 2030. Co-founder Pablo Moncada has
acknowledged this timeline is ambitious, but the intent is to set a pace that compels faster progress than what larger institutions typically deliver. The plan
includes fundraising for launch vehicle access, open-source hardware development, Earth-based habitat simulations, and the eventual transport of infrastructure
to the lunar surface. The funding model sets MoonDAO apart from traditional space organizations. Anyone worldwide can purchase $MOONEY tokens, lock them
for voting power, and participate in governance. The system uses quadratic voting to prevent any single large holder from controlling outcomes. Projects funded
through the platform share intellectual property with the community, creating a feedback loop where successful work generates revenue that funds future
missions.
Isla de LOBOS
To establish a decentralized and 100%
sustainable community through the L.O.B.O.S.
DAO
Isla de LOBOS is a project based in Uruguay that aims to build a decentralized and fully sustainable community governed through a DAO. The project centers on
1,000 unique NFTs called Voyagers, and holders of these NFTs collectively control and own the L.O.B.O.S. DAO, which serves as the vehicle for purchasing land
and managing the development of the community. The project describes itself as a protocol that speeds up the correct process of sustainable development for
civilization. The community is intended to be built in Uruguay, and the DAO structure means that major decisions about land acquisition, construction, governance
rules, and resource allocation are made collectively by the Voyager holders rather than by a central authority. The project sits at the intersection of web3
governance and physical community building. The idea is that by using blockchain-based ownership and decision-making tools, the community can operate with
full transparency and shared responsibility from day one, rather than relying on a traditional developer-led model where residents have little say in how things are
run. The sustainability aspect is core to the mission. The community is designed to be 100% sustainable, meaning it aims to produce its own energy, manage its
own waste, and minimize its environmental footprint. While the project is still in its early stages, the framework it has laid out represents a specific bet on how
decentralized tools can be applied to real-world land development and community governance. Uruguay was chosen in part because of its relatively progressive
stance on technology and its stable political environment, which makes it a practical location for experimental governance models.

Union X City
To build a human-first city operating system
leveraging frontier tech
Union X City is a network city project being built on Vancouver Island, Canada. It describes itself as a digital nomad-friendly, technology-integrated community
that combines urban planning with environmental sustainability. The project sits on 789 acres of undeveloped land and is backed by HF0 Residency and Martine

governance and infrastructure. The city is designed around walkable, human-scaled urbanism with features like crypto-native payments, regenerative homes built

digital systems for governance, resource management, and community coordination rather than relying on traditional municipal bureaucracy. The project has
attracted attention from Bloomberg and has held events in Vancouver featuring speakers on blockchain's real-world applications in Canada. The broader vision
positions Union X City as a blueprint for what the founders call an "inter-city network society," where multiple communities across different locations share
governance tools, economic systems, and cultural values while maintaining their own local character. The project emphasizes that traditional urban environments
often struggle with environmental degradation, social isolation, and unsustainable infrastructure, and that Union X City is designed from the ground up to address
these problems. The founding team led by Henry Faarup has focused on creating a community where remote workers and tech professionals can live in a setting

Itana by Talent Cities
Building a remote-work-friendly city in Nigeria's

Itana, formerly known as Talent City, is building Africa's first digital free zone within the Lekki Free Zone corridor in Lagos, Nigeria. Founded by Luqman Edu and
Iyinoluwa Aboyeji, the co-founder of Andela and Flutterwave, Itana has raised over $10 million and secured $2 million in pre-seed funding from LocalGlobe,

Itana is designed as a jurisdiction specifically for technology and service businesses. It offers business registration, tax incentives, banking access, and

modeled after jurisdictions like Delaware in the US, e-Estonia in Europe, and Dubai in Asia. Through its online platform called Itana Edge, international
businesses can register, access regulatory services, and tap into incentives designed for the digital economy. The first physical campus is planned to house
1,000 residents and 2,500 remote workers, with constant power, high-speed internet, and coworking spaces. Itana is also working with Nigeria's Ministry of
Industry and the National Talent Export Programme to create 100,000 jobs over five years and establish Nigeria as a leader in cross-border service delivery. The
project positions itself as a bridge between Nigerian talent and global markets, with a focus on keeping skilled professionals in the country while connecting them
to international employers.
League of Free Cities
Support localized autonomy of present and
aspiring Free Cities.
The League of Free Cities is an organization that supports the development and autonomy of present and aspiring free cities around the world. It operates under
the umbrella of the Free Cities Foundation, which promotes self-governing territories that uphold individual rights and freedoms. The concept of free cities draws
from a long historical tradition, from the Italian city-states and the Hanseatic League of Northern Europe to modern special economic zones and charter cities.
What distinguishes a free city from other types of autonomous zones is the emphasis on residential freedom, meaning these are not just business-friendly tax
havens but places designed to provide better lives for the people who actually live there. The League serves as a connective tissue between various projects that
fall under this broad category, including charter cities, free private cities, seasteads, intentional communities, and network states. It provides resources,
networking, and advocacy for projects at different stages of development, from early-concept communities to established jurisdictions like Prospera and Ciudad
Morazan in Honduras. The Foundation publishes research on governance models, hosts events, and connects founders with investors, policymakers, and
potential residents. The underlying philosophy is that competition between jurisdictions creates better governance outcomes, because people and businesses can
choose to move to places that serve them well, which in turn pressures other jurisdictions to improve. The League does not itself build or govern any city. Instead,
it acts as a support network and knowledge hub for the broader movement toward localized, voluntary, and freedom-oriented governance.
Oceanix
Creating new land for the coastal city of Busan,
Korea
Oceanix is a blue tech company founded in 2018 by Itai Madamombe and Marc Collins Chen to design and build floating cities for people to live sustainably on
the ocean. The company's flagship project is Oceanix Busan, the world's first prototype sustainable floating city, developed in partnership with UN-Habitat and the
Busan Metropolitan City in South Korea. The project was unveiled at the UN headquarters in New York in April 2022, with architectural design led by BIG (Bjarke
Ingels Group) and SAMOO (Samsung Group). Oceanix Busan consists of three interconnected floating platforms totaling 6.3 hectares, designed to house a
community of 12,000 people, with the potential to expand to over 20 platforms and 100,000 residents. Each platform serves a distinct purpose: residential living,
lodging for visitors, and a co-working and maritime research hub. The platforms connect to the mainland via bridges and are designed to rise and fall with
changing water levels, making them naturally resilient to sea level rise and flooding. The project integrates six systems for achieving a circular economy: zero
waste, closed-loop water, food production through urban farming, net-zero energy from floating and rooftop solar panels, clean mobility, and coastal habitat
regeneration. Each neighborhood treats and replenishes its own water and generates 100% of its operational energy on site. The broader problem Oceanix is
trying to solve is real and urgent. Two out of five people worldwide live within 100 kilometers of a coastline, and 90 percent of mega-cities are vulnerable to rising
sea levels. Flooding already destroys billions of dollars of infrastructure annually and displaces millions of people. Oceanix positions floating infrastructure as a
practical way for coastal cities to expand safely onto the water rather than continuing to build in flood-prone areas.

Connect global citizens with international

Metropolis Global is a company that partners with governments to develop modern legal, economic, and digital infrastructure. The company works closely with

Atlas Island
To create a floating city-state that upholds
individual sovereignty and the non-aggression

Atlas Island is a seasteading project founded in 2021 by a group of Seasteading Institute ambassadors, led by Mason, a physician by training. The project has
gathered roughly 400 online members committed to becoming citizens of a future seastead. Atlas Island takes a deliberately pragmatic, step-by-step approach to
building a floating community rather than attempting to create a massive floating city from day one. The plan involves five stages, starting with establishing marina
communities made up of individual vessels near onshore amenities, then gradually developing infrastructure and moving further offshore. The platforms are
envisioned to work like a Free Private City, where agreements between individuals and service providers are laid out in explicit contracts. Rather than declaring
sovereignty, which has proven legally risky for other seasteading projects, Atlas Island plans to operate under a flag of convenience, similar to how cruise ships
work. This provides near-complete autonomy while staying within international law. The organization is structured around local associations that operate like
yacht clubs, with the first club being set up in Florida. Each local association chooses how to organize itself, with guidance from the main board. The project
combines ideas from seasteading, free private cities, agorism, and cryptocurrency. Atlas Island is motivated by the observation that the biggest predictor of
someone's economic success is the country where they were born, and aims to provide an alternative for people whose potential is limited by the government
they happen to live under.
Urbit
To create a new decentralized computing
platform where users have complete ownership
and control over their data, identities, and
services.
Urbit is a project building a new decentralized computing platform where users have complete ownership and control over their data, identities, and services.
Unlike the current internet, where your data lives on servers owned by companies like Google, Facebook, or Amazon, Urbit gives each user their own personal
server, a small piece of the network that they fully own and control. Your Urbit server runs your applications, stores your data, and handles your communications,
all without relying on any centralized service. The system uses a unique identity layer where each person has a permanent, cryptographic identity tied to their
server. These identities are scarce and owned like property, meaning nobody can take them away or censor them. The network is designed so that users
communicate directly with each other, peer-to-peer, rather than routing everything through corporate intermediaries. Urbit was founded by Curtis Yarvin and has
been in development for over a decade. The project has built its own programming language (Hoon), its own operating system (Arvo), and its own networking
protocol. The learning curve is steep, and the community tends to attract people with strong technical backgrounds and an interest in digital sovereignty. The
practical appeal of Urbit is that it offers an alternative to the current model where a handful of tech companies control the infrastructure that most people depend
on for communication, social media, file storage, and other basic digital services. On Urbit, if you disagree with how a service works, you can modify it yourself or
switch to an alternative without losing your identity, your data, or your social connections.
Catawba (CDEZ)
To create a regulatory framework that supports
digital entrepreneurs and innovation in
emerging technologies.
The Catawba Digital Economic Zone is the first jurisdiction within the United States created specifically for blockchain, fintech, and digital asset companies. It was
established in February 2022 through a vote by the Catawba Indian Nation, a federally recognized Native American tribe located in the Carolinas with a 700-acre
reservation and over 3,000 citizens. The Zone operates under the sovereign authority of the Catawba Nation, which gives it the legal ability to create its own civil
laws and regulatory framework independent of state and federal regulations in certain areas. The CDEZ allows companies from anywhere in the world to
incorporate as LLCs or C-Corps and operate under laws specifically written for digital businesses. The regulatory framework defines blockchain, digital assets,
digital securities, virtual currencies, and NFTs, and is based in part on the work of Wyoming policymakers but with improvements tailored to the digital economy.
The Zone has also passed regulations allowing DAOs to register as recognized legal entities, which is something few jurisdictions anywhere in the world currently
offer. Unlike many special economic zones that rely on tax breaks to attract businesses, the CDEZ's primary value is its legal structure. The laws are designed to
provide clarity and certainty for businesses that operate in areas where traditional regulation is ambiguous or hostile. The Zone Authority, which governs the
CDEZ, has four of its five seats occupied by Catawba officials, with the fifth seat held by the management company. The project is expected to create local
employment opportunities for Catawba Nation members and bring technology companies into the community as mentors and partners.

Closer
The operating system for sovereign
communities.
Closer is a platform that provides management and governance tools for regenerative, land-based communities. The project describes itself as the operating
system for sovereign communities, and it offers software that handles day-to-day operations like guest bookings, volunteer stays, resident accommodations,
event management, space coordination, and resource sharing, all through a single integrated system. For communities that are ready for more advanced
governance, Closer also offers web3 features including native tokens, proof of presence tracking, proof of participation (called proof of sweat), and decentralized
decision-making tools. The platform is built around the idea that regenerative communities, meaning communities focused on restoring ecosystems and building
sustainable local economies, spend too much time on administrative and governance overhead. By providing a ready-made operating system, Closer lets these
communities focus on their actual work rather than getting bogged down in spreadsheets and coordination problems. The platform connects a network of
regenerative communities around the world, creating a shared infrastructure layer that individual projects can plug into. Closer has received grants from the
Gitcoin ecosystem and the Regen Network, and it operates as a DAO with its own treasury. The pricing model ties transaction fees to a community's verified
regenerative practices, meaning the more environmental impact a community creates, the lower their fees become. The project sits at the intersection of the ReFi
(regenerative finance) movement and the practical needs of physical communities trying to organize themselves effectively.
Kleros
To democratize access to justice using
cutting-edge technologies.
Kleros is a decentralized dispute resolution protocol built on Ethereum that uses crowdsourced jurors and economic incentives to settle disagreements. The
system works as an autonomous third party that can arbitrate disputes in any kind of contract, from simple escrow transactions to complex commercial
agreements. When a dispute arises, a pool of jurors is randomly selected from token holders who have staked PNK (Kleros' native token) in the relevant court
category. Jurors review the evidence submitted by both parties and vote on the outcome. Those who vote with the majority earn arbitration fees and tokens, while
those who vote against the majority lose a portion of their stake. This incentive structure is designed to encourage honest, well-reasoned voting. Kleros was
co-founded by Federico Ast, who holds a PhD and has written extensively on decentralized justice. The project was incubated by Thomson Reuters and received
the European Union's Blockchains for Social Good award from the Horizon 2020 research programme. In practice, Kleros has been used primarily for curated
token lists and compliance verification, though its potential applications extend to e-commerce disputes, insurance claims, freelancing disagreements, and
content moderation. A notable milestone came in 2020 when a Kleros decision was recognized and enforced in a Mexican court as part of a real estate leasing
contract, demonstrating that blockchain-based dispute resolution can have real legal standing within existing judicial systems. The platform is open and
permissionless, meaning anyone can serve as a juror by staking tokens in the courts they feel qualified to judge. Stanford Law School has published a case study
analyzing Kleros as a socio-legal experiment in decentralized justice.
Porta Norte
Building a European town integrated with
nature
Porta Norte is a master-planned walkable community being built in the northern zone of Panama City, Panama. Spanning 260 hectares, the project is led by CEO
Henry Faarup Humbert and developed by Grupo Colonias, a company founded in 1984 that pioneered gated communities in Panama. Porta Norte is designed
around New Urbanism principles, meaning it prioritizes pedestrian accessibility, mixed-use development, and integration with the natural environment rather than
car-dependent suburban sprawl. The community is planned to include residential, commercial, and institutional lots, along with sports facilities, parks, plazas, and
direct access to natural rivers and trails. The development preserves centennial trees and native species to support local wildlife, and buildings use natural
ventilation and local materials. The project is described as eight times the size of Panama City's historic Casco Viejo district and is inspired by the walkable,
community-centered character of European towns. Residents will have access to shops, schools, and workplaces within walking distance, and the street network
is designed around micro-mobility rather than cars. The project emphasizes social interaction as a deliberate design goal, with public squares, community spaces,
and shared gardens intended to combat the isolation that remote workers often experience. The broader vision is to create a model for urban development in
Panama that proves you can have the conveniences of city life while living in a setting that feels more like a village surrounded by nature. Construction is
underway, with the first phase of the project already in development.
Gelephu Mindfulness
City
Integrate economic growth with mindfulness
and sustainability.
Gelephu Mindfulness City is a Special Administrative Region in southern Bhutan, announced by King Jigme Khesar Namgyel Wangchuck on National Day in
December 2023. The project covers 2,500 square kilometers, roughly three times the size of Singapore, and is located on the India-Bhutan border in a
strategically important corridor connecting South and Southeast Asia. The city operates under a "One Country, Two Systems" framework with its own executive,
legislative, and judicial powers, separate from Bhutan's existing laws. The masterplan was designed by BIG (Bjarke Ingels Group) in collaboration with Arup and
Cistri, and features eleven mandala-inspired neighborhoods organized around the 35 rivers and streams that flow through the site. Buildings will be low-rise,
using local materials like wood, stone, and bamboo, and the development preserves biodiversity corridors for elephants and other wildlife. Bhutan is the world's
only carbon-negative country, and the city is designed to maintain that status through hydroelectric power, clean mobility, and carbon-neutral building design. The
project received endorsement from Nobel laureates Joseph Stiglitz and Michael Spence, architect Bjarke Ingels, and Snap CEO Evan Spiegel at the Bhutan
Innovation Forum in 2024. Dasho Dr. Lotay Tshering, Bhutan's former Prime Minister, was appointed Governor. Joi Ito, former Director of MIT Media Lab, chairs
the investment corporation. The city is organized around seven economic clusters: spirituality, health and wellness, education, technology and innovation,
sustainable industry, culture, and finance. In December 2025, Bhutan launched a gold-backed digital token called TER on the Solana blockchain, issued through
the GMC framework and managed by DK Bank, Bhutan's first licensed digital bank.

Plumia
Building a borderless society for digital nomads
Plumia is a nonprofit think tank exploring the future of citizenship in a borderless world, with the long-term goal of building an internet country for digital nomads.
The project was conceived during a hackathon at SafetyWing, an insurance company for remote workers and travelers whose mission is to build a global social
safety net. Lauren Razavi, a British-Iranian author of "Global Natives," served as Plumia's executive director and has been the project's most prominent voice,
describing it as a "moonshot mission" to offer citizenship as a service. Plumia's 10-year horizon goal is to provide the full functionality of a nation-state in a
borderless, portable format, including health insurance, income protection, pensions, and dispute resolution that people normally access through national
governments. The project has been in discussions with multiple governments about partnerships, including publicly with Montenegro. Plumia publishes
Borderless, a weekly newsletter that grew from 2,000 readers in January 2024 to over 65,000 by December of that year, covering news relevant to digital
nomads, remote workers, expats, and global citizens. Through its partnership with Interintellect, Plumia hosts a monthly salon series called "Building a Different
Network State" that brings together thinkers, builders, and policymakers. Razavi has likened national citizenship and tax to a subscription that is very hard to
cancel, saying that people are enrolled based on the coincidence of their birthplace, which does not work in the 21st century. The project sits within SafetyWing's
broader ecosystem, which already provides travel and health insurance to nomads and is expanding into income protection and pension services.
RNS.ID
Digital identity backed by the sovereign state of
Palau
RNS.ID (Root Name System) is the official technology provider for the Republic of Palau's Digital Residency Program, making it the world's first sovereign-backed
blockchain-native digital identity platform. The program was created through the Digital Residency Act, signed into law by Palau's President Surangel S. Whipps
Jr. The platform allows global citizens from 138 countries, including the United States, to apply for digital residency in Palau for $248 per year. Approved
applicants receive both a physical government-issued ID card and a digital identity on the blockchain in the form of an NFT. The application process is done
entirely online, with no travel required, and approval can take as little as a few minutes after passing identity verification and anti-money laundering checks. As of
mid-2024, RNS.ID had issued over 19,000 digital residencies. The Palau ID has been used for practical purposes including hotel check-ins, flight boarding,
pharmacy visits, crypto exchange KYC, and even bank account applications. The first Legal Decentralized ID was minted on Ethereum in September 2023, with
Ethereum co-founder Vitalik Buterin in attendance. RNS.ID later expanded to the Solana blockchain, marking the first time a sovereign nation issued legal identity
on that network. Digital residents of Palau benefit from zero tax on non-Palauan income. The program represents a new model where a small sovereign nation
uses digital infrastructure to extend its economic relevance beyond its physical borders, similar to Estonia's e-Residency program but with a blockchain-native
approach.
Traditional Dream
Factory
Traditional Dream Factory (TDF) is a regenerative coliving village located in Abela, a small Portuguese village of 400 inhabitants in the Alentejo region, about 90
minutes from Lisbon and 30 minutes from the coast. The project transformed a former 1972 poultry factory into a functioning community and sits on 25 hectares
of land with hills, valleys, protected oak trees, a flowing river, and an old farmhouse. TDF is the first physical location within the OASA network, a Swiss non-profit
association that holds land in conservation and offers it as commons to community members. The project operates as a DAO, described as one of Europe's first
operational land-based DAOs, and issues the $TDF token. One token equals one night per year of accommodation access, permanently, and token holders vote
on development plans, executive team decisions, and community rules. As of late 2025, TDF has 280 co-owners, has hosted 14 events with 358 participants, and
raised 106,000 euros directly from its community. The village has planted over 333 trees using syntropic agroforestry systems with 65-plus tree species, stored
over 1.2 million liters of rainwater, and keeps 50% of its land as wild habitat supporting 40-plus bird species. Physical infrastructure includes a cafe, event hall,
workshops, a pool, a propagation greenhouse, chicken systems, and a mushroom farm. The community welcomed its first baby in September 2025. TDF is
raising 1.2 million euros for the next construction phase: 13 en-suite coliving rooms, 4 studios, a farm-to-table restaurant, and a 200 kW solar park expansion.

Edge Esmeralda
Edge Esmeralda is a month-long popup village organized by Edge City and Devon Zuegel's Esmeralda Institute in Healdsburg, California. The first event took
place in June 2024 and brought together roughly 1,300 participants who lived, worked, and collaborated in the small Sonoma County town of 11,000 people. The
gathering was designed as both a standalone event and a prototype for a permanent new town called Esmeralda that Zuegel is developing near Cloverdale,
California, where she has purchased an option on land. Of the 2024 programming, 93% was self-organized by attendees rather than the organizing team. Events
ranged from AI-wearable soldering sessions and neurotech workshops to running clubs, night markets, and art exhibitions. Notable programs included a longevity
research weekend organized by Laura Deming, a cryptography deep-dive week co-sponsored by Uniswap Foundation and Ethereum Foundation, and an
abundance-focused unconference. Real outcomes emerged: Constellation, a neurotech startup, collected neural data on-site, closed their lead investor, and
raised $10 million during the event. The Edge Esmeralda 2025 event ran from May 24 to June 21, and Edge Esmeralda 2026 is already announced for May 30 to
June 27. Zuegel's vision for the permanent Esmeralda community is inspired by the historic Chautauqua movement: a walkable town for multigenerational
families focused on lifelong learning, car-light living, and independence for children. Edge City, the co-organizing partner, also runs popup villages in other
locations including Thailand (Edge City Lanna) and has built EdgeOS, an open-source operating system for network societies.

Akiya Collective is a social research and development lab focused on human flourishing, based in rural Japan. The project revitalizes akiya, which are abandoned
homes that exist in large numbers across the Japanese countryside due to decades of rural depopulation and urban migration. Japan has an estimated 8 to 9
million vacant homes, and this number is growing as the population ages and shrinks. Akiya Collective takes these abandoned structures and transforms them
into experimental spaces, art installations, and residencies for artists, researchers, and community builders. The project functions as both a practical intervention
in rural decline and a laboratory for testing new ideas about community, creativity, and how people can live well together. By bringing international visitors and
residents into small Japanese towns, the Collective creates cultural exchange that benefits both the newcomers and the existing local communities. The
residencies provide affordable workspace and housing for creative professionals who might otherwise be priced out of major cities, while the renovations bring
economic activity and fresh energy to towns that have been losing population for decades. The project treats each abandoned home as an opportunity to ask
fundamental questions about what spaces people need to do their best work and build meaningful relationships.

governance can emerge organically from creative expression rather than requiring massive capital investment or complex legal frameworks.

ZuAfrique is building the foundation for Africa's largest on-chain movement. The project brings together global builders and African innovators to create what it
describes as a new era of trust, ownership, and abundance. ZuAfrique is part of the Zuzalu family of popup village events, adapted specifically for the African

conditions where decentralized alternatives to traditional financial and governance systems can provide immediate practical value.
The Mu

permissionless, dev-focused, pop-up cities to

The Mu is bringing developer-focused, cypherpunk, permissionless popup cities to the world. The project targets software developers and technologists who
identify with the cypherpunk movement, which emphasizes privacy, cryptography, and individual autonomy through technology. The Mu organizes temporary
physical gatherings where these values are put into practice, creating environments where participants can build tools, share knowledge, and form communities
around their shared commitment to privacy-preserving technology and decentralized systems.

ZuJapan
Permanent Zuzalu coliving community in rural
Japan buying abandoned homes and making
art
ZuJapan is a permanent Zuzalu coliving community based in rural Japan. The project purchases and renovates abandoned homes (akiya) and transforms them
into coliving spaces for a community focused on art, technology, and alternative living. Japan's rural areas have millions of abandoned homes due to decades of
urban migration and population decline, creating an opportunity for projects like ZuJapan to acquire property at very low cost and breathe new life into
depopulated towns. The permanent nature of ZuJapan distinguishes it from temporary popup villages, as the community maintains year-round housing and a
stable core of residents.
Crecimiento
A movement making Argentina the Crypto
Capital of the World. Bring Argentina onchain.
Crecimiento is a movement working to make Argentina the crypto capital of the world, with a specific focus on bringing Argentina on-chain. Argentina has been
one of the most enthusiastic adopters of cryptocurrency globally, driven by chronic peso devaluation, capital controls, and economic instability that make crypto a
practical tool for preserving savings rather than just a speculative investment. Crecimiento builds on this existing adoption by organizing community, attracting
investment, and developing infrastructure that makes it easier for Argentine businesses and individuals to operate on-chain. The name means "growth" in
Spanish, reflecting the project's focus on economic development through crypto adoption.

Remote Life
Noma Collective describes itself as a minimum viable society experiment, formed by the merger of two major digital nomad communities to create more
opportunities for location-independent workers. The project is associated with Remote Life and brings together experienced digital nomads who have been living
and working across borders for years. By merging existing communities rather than starting from scratch, Noma Collective benefits from established networks,
tested practices, and a membership base that already understands the challenges and rewards of nomadic life. The "minimum viable society" framing borrows
from startup language to suggest an experimental approach to community building, starting with the smallest set of shared services and governance structures
that can support a functioning community and iterating from there.
Zanzalu
Our mission is to help build the innovation
ecosystem in East Africa. I
Zanzalu's mission is to help build the innovation ecosystem in East Africa. The project is part of the Zuzalu family of popup village events, adapted for the East
African context. East Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania, has a growing tech ecosystem with significant potential but limited access to the global networks of
capital, expertise, and talent that are concentrated in a few cities worldwide. Zanzalu aims to bridge this gap by bringing international builders and investors
directly into the East African ecosystem for extended periods of collaboration and community building.
Frontier Tower
Transforming a 16-floor tower into a vertical
village for frontier-tech pioneers in San
Francisco. The blueprint for an inter-city
network society III
Frontier Tower is transforming a 16-floor tower in San Francisco into a vertical village for frontier-tech pioneers. The project takes an existing building and
converts it into a dedicated community space where technology builders can live, work, and collaborate under one roof. The vertical village format is a practical
innovation for expensive urban real estate markets, where acquiring large parcels of land for a traditional community is impossible. By stacking community
vertically in a tower, the project creates density and proximity in a city where those qualities are expensive but extremely valuable for the kind of intensive
collaboration that frontier technology development requires. The project positions itself as a blueprint for an inter-city network society, suggesting that the model
could be replicated in towers in other cities to create a connected network of vertical tech communities.
